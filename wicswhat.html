"Programming is a skill best acquired by practice and example rather than from books." - Alan Turing
While a part of computer science is programming, that’s not the bulk of it.
Computer science means working with others to use logic and computational thinking to help solve real-world problems. It is about helping people’s lives become, easier, safer, and over-all better. If the media and schools would teach that definition of computer science rather than just programming, many more kids would be interested in learning about it. Computer science does not just affect cyberspace but also meat space. Not many people talk about how the internet connects all of us through a bunch of cables all along the coast or how all of the information stored in the cloud is actually in an incredibly large warehouse with stacks of hard drives that are about five feet in width and length and about three and a half feet tall. All of computer science is not on a computer but has real-life applications that are not talked about in schools or among media. The truth is, the internet is so fagile and can be broken or interfiered with so easily. For example, The entire country of Armenia lost internet for about 5 hours becuase an old woman accidentally cut the internet cables that run through the country while looking for copper.
